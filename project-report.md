## 💠 Task 4

A common example of poor digital communication is when a student posts inappropriate or offensive content on social media without considering its impact. For instance, a student might share a joke or comment that is misunderstood and ends up offending others, leading to conflicts or even disciplinary action. In some cases, such posts can harm a student’s reputation and affect future opportunities, such as internships or placements.

This situation could have been avoided if the student had thought carefully before posting and considered how others might interpret the content. Practicing responsible digital behavior, maintaining respect, and reviewing posts before sharing are essential steps. Additionally, understanding the importance of a professional online presence can help students avoid such problems and use social media more positively and effectively.

## 💠 Task 5

While researching cybercrime, what surprised me the most was how easily people can fall victim to online fraud, especially UPI scams, simply by approving a payment request or sharing an OTP without realizing the consequences. Many of these attacks do not involve complex hacking but rely on human error and lack of awareness. I also found it alarming that fraudsters use simple techniques like fake calls or messages to create urgency and pressure victims into making quick decisions.

As a result of this research, one habit I will personally change is being more cautious before approving any online transaction or clicking on links. I will always verify the source of any request and avoid acting in a hurry. This awareness will help me stay safer online and protect my personal and financial information.
